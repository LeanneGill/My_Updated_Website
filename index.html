Yoga Explained

Yoga is an ancient form of exercise that focuses on strength, flexibility and breathing to boost physical and mental wellbeing. The main components of yoga are postures (a series of movements designed to increase strength and flexibility) and breathing. The practice originated in India and has been adapted in other countries in a variety of ways. The benefits of yoga can be found here

Where Did Yoga Originate?

The beginnings of Yoga were developed by the Indus-Sarasvati civilization in Northern India over 5,000 years. Patanjali is believed to be the father of yoga.

Vinyasa

“Vinyasa” means linking breath with movement. The postures are usually done in a flowing sequence, or "vinyasa flow." The fluid movements can be memorized and done as a moving meditation, almost like a dance.

Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga means “eight limbs” and encompasses a yogic lifestyle. Most people identify Ashtanga as traditional Indian yoga. Like Vinyasa yoga, the Ashtanga yoga asanas (postures) synchronize breath with movement as you move through a series of postures.

Iyengar Yoga

The emphasis on this practice is alignment in the asanas using breath control through pranayama and the use of props (bolsters, blankets, blocks and straps.) This style of yoga is usually taught without music and at a slower pace designed to assist students to get deeper into the postures.

PowerYoga

The Ashtanga yoga poses are performed more quickly and with added core exercises and upper body work.

Yin Yoga

Yin yoga is a meditative practice that allows your body to become comfortable in a pose without doing any work (strength). It is also called Taoist yoga, and focuses on lengthening the connective tissues within the body.

Reasons to Pratice Yoga

Yoga Can help you...

  • Reduce your risk for injury.
  • Reduce stress.
  • Gain strength and stamina.
  • Improve balance and stability.
  • Improve posture.
